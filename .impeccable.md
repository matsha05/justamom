## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are Christian mothers, especially women carrying the emotional and spiritual weight of ordinary motherhood and looking for grounded encouragement that does not talk down to them. Secondary users are event organizers, editors, and agents evaluating Lizi Shaw's writing and speaking.

Core jobs to be done:
- Read short and long-form notes with ease and trust.
- Subscribe to a quiet, twice-monthly newsletter for ongoing encouragement.
- Understand Lizi's message, current project, and speaking themes quickly.
- Reach out for speaking, literary, or editorial inquiries without friction.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel warm, steady, trustworthy, editorial, and Scripture-rooted without drifting into vague lifestyle-blog softness. The voice is calm, honest, gently confident, and human.

Three-word personality:
- Warm
- Grounded
- Trustworthy

Emotional goals:
- Quiet confidence
- Relief from cultural noise
- Spiritual steadiness

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is warm editorial: soft paper tones, restrained accent color, long-form readability, and a composed literary feel. It should feel more like a thoughtful print essay or author site than a trendy content brand.

Reference qualities already present in project materials:
- Editorial warmth without feeling dated
- Trust-first conversion surfaces
- Clear long-form reading rhythm
- Premium but understated presentation

Anti-direction:
- Lifestyle-blog vagueness
- Trendy AI-generated aesthetics
- Loud, glossy, or performative design

### Design Principles
1. Trust before novelty: every surface should reduce noise and increase clarity.
2. Editorial readability is a feature: typography, spacing, and contrast must support sustained reading.
3. Conversion should feel invitational, not sales-driven.
4. Warmth needs structure: softness should be balanced by hierarchy and precision.
5. The interface should honor the emotional weight of the subject matter without becoming heavy-handed.
