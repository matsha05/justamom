About Lizi

Hi, I'm Lizi Shaw.

I’m a wife, mom of three young kids, and someone who deeply understands how overwhelming modern motherhood can feel. Between cultural pressure, information overload, and the everyday demands of life, it’s easy to lose sight of what really matters. That’s why I’m passionate about helping women cut through the noise and live with clarity, confidence, and peace rooted in Biblical truth.

I don’t claim to have it all figured out, but I’ve learned (sometimes the hard way) that small, faithful steps in the right direction make a big difference. I love speaking to women in every stage of life and helping them rediscover joy in the simple and sacred work they’re already doing.

Whether I’m encouraging a room full of moms or having a quiet conversation with a friend, my hope is always the same: to point women back to God’s goodness and remind them that they’re not alone in this.

Let’s walk it out together. With grace, grit, and a little laughter along the way.

Third-person bio

Short bio (for programs or quick use):

Lizi Shaw is a writer and speaker who helps overwhelmed moms trade cultural pressure for a steady, gospel-rooted identity. Drawing from Scripture and her own story of wrestling with the labels “just a mom” and “do it all,” she invites women to step out of the noise and remember who God says they are. Lizi lives in Colorado with her husband and their three children.

Longer bio (for websites and promo pages):

Lizi Shaw is a writer and speaker who encourages real moms living in the tension between “just a mom” and “do it all.” With warmth, honesty, and a deep love for Scripture, she helps women untangle the cultural lies that tie their worth to performance and points them back to the Genesis story where God names women as image bearers before He ever gives them a role or job.

Through her writing and speaking, Lizi comes alongside weary mothers who feel like they have lost themselves in the swirl of diapers, school drop-offs, and expectations they can never quite meet. She offers a better story: that motherhood is a beautiful calling, but not the source of a woman’s identity.

Lizi lives in Colorado with her husband and their three kids. When she is not loading the dishwasher for the third time in a day, you can find her scribbling notes for her book, gathering with friends, or quietly savoring a hot cup of coffee before the house wakes up.
